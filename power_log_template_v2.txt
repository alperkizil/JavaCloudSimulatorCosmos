Power Log Template v2
=====================

This text file is a human-readable export of the Excel template 'power_log_template_v2.xlsx'.
It preserves the two sheets: 'How to use' and 'Power Log'.

How to Use
----------
- Basics: Fill Total Time (s) and Total Energy (kWh). Average Power and Energy (J) compute
  automatically.
- Idle: Create one 'IDLE' row with the same peripherals connected. Copy its Average Power into 'Idle
  Avg (W)' for all workload rows.
- Incremental: Incremental columns remove idle power, so you compare the workload itself rather than
  your PC existing.
- Normalization: Use 'Units Processed' for images, tokens, frames, iterations, etc. Energy per Unit
  and Throughput will compute.
- Meter notes: If your meter provides Power Factor, Voltage, Current averages, log them. Otherwise
  leave blank.
- Repeatability: Warm up the workload, run at least 3 times, and use the 'Runs' and 'Run Notes' to
  track repeats and quirks.
- Sanity check: Average Power (W) ≈ (kWh * 1000) / (Time(h)). Energy(J) = kWh * 3,600,000.

Power Log Columns
-----------------
Likely inputs you record:
  • Workload
  • Date
  • System Notes (CPU/GPU/PSU/Driver)
  • Meter Model
  • Total Time (s)
  • Total Energy (kWh)
  • Units Processed
  • Runs
  • Run Notes
  • Power Factor (avg)
  • Voltage (avg, V)
  • Current (avg, A)
  • Peak Power (W)
  • Lowest Power (W)

Likely calculated by spreadsheet formulas:
  • Energy (J)
  • Average Power (W)
  • Idle Avg (W)
  • Incremental Avg Power (W)
  • Incremental Energy (J)
  • Energy per Unit (J/unit)
  • Throughput (units/s)
